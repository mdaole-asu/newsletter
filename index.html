Carl,
Cash. Moolah. Dough. Benjamins. Scratch. Whatever term you use, money is an important necessity and will become even more important as you begin college. As the countdown to ASU continues (only two months until fall classes start), we¿re offering you advice about money: how to earn it, how to save it and how to spend it responsibly. Follow these tips to help you get more skrilla, coinage, clams ¿ whatever! ¿ into your pocket.
 
Earn money by working on campus
 
ASU offers more than $31 million in student employment, not including Federal Work-Study. Have you searched ASU's part-time student job opportunities? Studies show that students who work up to 20 hours per week while attending college do as well or better in school than their classmates who don¿t work. You'll be more connected, learn to manage your time better, gain helpful experience to add to your professional resume ¿ all while earning a paycheck that you can use to fund your education.

And working on campus definitely has its perks. Check out this video featuring a few of the 11,000+ students who work at ASU during the school year.
 
How to stay debt free
 
Whether you¿ll be managing money on your own for the first time or you just need some tips to become better at it, we have a tool that will help.

ASU's student budget worksheet allows you to view your ASU costs (tuition, books, housing and meals) and deduct any financial aid you may have received. You¿ll instantly see if there are any funds left over to apply to everyday expenses for the next year. If it looks like things will be tight, you may want to consider other resources like student employment.
 
Top 5 money-saving tips
 
  1. Pizza does not qualify as a necessity (unless maybe you¿re pulling an all-night study session).
  2. If you¿re spending more money than you¿re making, you must spend less or make more. Look into getting an on-campus job.
  3. Treat your budget the same way you treat your classes ¿ be organized, keep up with it, try not to make any errors and ask for help if you need it.
  4. Look for discounts whenever you buy something. (Hint: Use your Sun Card around campus for extra discounts.)
  5. Credit cards should be used only in emergencies and to scrape the ice off your windshield on cold mornings in January. (Yes, it really gets that cold here ¿ once or twice a year.)

Say cheese: your official ASU ID card
 
As a new student, you can choose between two Sun Devil ID card options: the basic Sun Card, which gives you access to buildings and athletic events, and works as a student ID for exams and advising; or the Pitchfork ID MasterCard® Check Card, which is an all-in-one student ID, MasterCard® check card and building access card. (You can receive this card by opening an official Sun Devil checking account through MidFirst Bank.) Both cards will come loaded with your meal plan and M&G dollars.
 
Attend a Sun Devil Send-Off
 
Like any other soon-to-be Sun Devil, you might be feeling a little nervous about the transition from high school to college. Don¿t worry, it¿s completely normal. One of the best ways to get prepared is to have all your questions answered ¿ which is why we host Sun Devil Send-Offs around the country. You can ask ASU alumni anything you want, from the best place to study or hang out on campus to tips on where to eat. (You could even find out where the Secret Garden is at ASU!) It is also an opportunity to make friends with other incoming students before coming to college.
